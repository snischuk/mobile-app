The folowing list is a time line of Henry Ford

  • 1863 - born on a farm in Springwells Township, Michigan.
  • 1879 - He left home to work as an apprentice machinist in Detroit.
  • 1882 - He returned to Dearborn to work on the family farm, where he became adept at operating the Westinghouse portable steam engine.
  • 1885 - Ford repaired an Otto engine
  • 1887 - He built a four-cycle model with a one-inch bore and a three-inch stroke.
  • 1890 - Ford started work on a two-cylinder engine.
  • 1891 - Ford became an engineer with the Edison Illuminating Company of Detroit.
  • 1901 - Murphy and other stockholders in the Detroit Automobile Company formed the Henry Ford Company
  • 1908 - The Model T debuted
  • 1918 - Half of all cars in the United States were Model Ts
  • 1922 - Ford also purchased Lincoln Motor Co.
  • 1932 - Ford was manufacturing one-third of the world's automobiles.
  • 1943 - The elderly and ailing Henry Ford decided to assume the presidency
  • 1947 - He died on April 7 of a cerebral hemorrhage at Fair Lane, his estate in Dearborn, at the age of 83.
